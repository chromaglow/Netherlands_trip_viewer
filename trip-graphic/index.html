Housing Decision Research  ·  Three Cities  ·  May 2026

Three Cities.
One Decision.

Ten days on the ground across the Netherlands. Amsterdam, Rotterdam, The Hague, Haarlem, Utrecht, Leiden, Delft. Every city got a fair shot — walked the neighborhoods at different hours, tested the transit, tracked down the canal streets, looked at the actual housing stock.

The bar is genuinely high everywhere. This is not a country with bad options. What it comes down to isn't quality — it's fit. Three cities survived every filter. This is what the data says about what separates them.

24 absolute listings. Lombok and Wittevrouwen have high concentrations of independent landlords — the ones willing to negotiate on pets rather than auto-reject.

⚠ Pararius confirmed to have no pet filter as of May 2, 2026. All pet data from Funda keyword search on "huisdieren" within listing descriptions only. These are strict lower-bound estimates. Actual pet-friendly supply is likely higher, particularly from private landlords who communicate policy verbally rather than in listing text.

Delft's 44% rate is the highest of the three. Utrecht's 16% rate means 24 options. In a search with a hard constraint, absolute numbers beat percentages every time.

Strategy: lead with the pugs in every inquiry. Private landlords in older neighborhoods respond far better than agencies managing new-build stock. Utrecht's depth gives you room to work that strategy at scale — and to walk away from a no.

05
Where You'd Actually Live

Not city-level.
Block-level.

Canal proximity, old building stock, independent landlords, dog-friendly streets. Two candidates per city that survived every filter.

Utrecht — Top Pick
Lombok
multicultural independent landlords Kanaalstraat life canal access pug-negotiable

Affordable by Utrecht standards, genuinely diverse, strong street-level community. Old housing stock means private landlords — negotiation on pets is possible. The kind of neighborhood with a butcher, a Turkish grocer, and a coffee spot that knows your name. Canal access at Leidsche Rijn. This is the top candidate.

Utrecht — Strong Second
Wittevrouwen
village-in-city feel Wilhelminapark young families leafy streets excellent morning walks

Quieter, leafier, more residential. Wilhelminapark is a 10-minute walk — perfect morning pug loop. Nachtegaalstraat has genuine local restaurants. Slightly pricier than Lombok but still independent housing stock with real character. Feels like a smaller city within the city.

Leiden — Top Candidate
Centrum
canal-dense historic core walkable expensive & scarce student competition

Genuinely beautiful. Dense canal network, compact medieval streets, real Dutch architecture. But supply is extremely tight — most old-town rentals move within days. 24,000 Leiden University students compete for the same stock. Pet-friendly private landlords exist but require patience and very good timing.

Leiden — Practical Alternative
Transvaalwijk
residential more affordable canal proximity less character

The practical fallback within Leiden. More supply, slightly lower rents than Centrum, still within cycling range of everything. Not the canal-life dream, but genuinely liveable. Better odds of finding a pet-friendly listing than old-town Leiden — but you're conceding what made Leiden appealing in the first place.

Delft — Top Candidate
Binnenstad
canal-dense Golden Age streets walkable 0% new construction razor-thin supply

The most purely characterful old town of the three cities. 100% existing stock, genuine 17th-century streetscape, dense canal network. But the whole city of Delft has 18 active Funda listings. Binnenstad may have 3–5 at any moment. Beautiful — and functionally inaccessible on a fixed arrival timeline.

Delft — Best Realistic Option
Vrijenban
pre-war stock (40%+) north of centre diverse community Delftse Hout on doorstep

North of the centre, borders the Delftse Hout recreation area (400ha). More supply than Binnenstad, older private-landlord housing stock. The best realistic chance of finding a 2BR with character in Delft. The park access for the pugs is genuinely exceptional — if one comes available.

Canal character — the real comparison

Utrecht's Oudegracht has canal-level wharves at two levels — unique in Europe. Delft's Binnenstad has the densest canal network. Leiden's Centrum sits in between. All three deliver canal-life. The difference is supply, not scenery.

The scarcity problem stated plainly

Delft Binnenstad is as beautiful as anything in the Netherlands. Finding a pet-friendly 2BR there when you need to land within a specific window — arriving internationally, registering a business, two dogs — is a different problem than finding a beautiful city.

Lombok gives you what Leiden Centrum and Delft Binnenstad promise — canal proximity, old building character, independent landlords — without the scarcity that makes both of them so frustrating to actually land in on a fixed timeline.

The Delft canal dream is the most beautiful of the three. It's also the one most likely to still be a dream six months after you start looking. The Leiden canal dream is real — so is competing with 24,000 students for three available old-town rentals at any given moment.

Utrecht isn't the compromise. It's the answer. Delft is the most beautiful city in this comparison — and the one most likely to leave you still searching six months after you arrive. Leiden's affordability story was always thinner than it looked, and its rents are now rising faster than anywhere else in the Netherlands.

Utrecht has the housing stock, the neighborhoods, the business infrastructure, the dog-walking parks, and the private landlords who'll actually talk about the pugs. Lombok gives you canal proximity and old building character without the scarcity that makes Delft Binnenstad and Leiden Centrum so hard to land in on a fixed timeline. The data lined up — and so did ten days on the ground.

Methodology Notes

Funda uses 2+ kamers (rooms including living room). Pararius uses 2+ slaapkamers (bedrooms). These filters are not identical — a Funda 2-kamer is 1 bedroom + living room, while a Pararius 2-slaapkamer is a true 2-bedroom unit. Pararius prices skew higher as a result. Combined supply totals are estimates after estimated deduplication.

Pet-friendly data from Funda only. Pararius pet filter confirmed absent as of May 2, 2026. Funda keyword search on "huisdieren" within listing description text is a strict lower bound — landlords who communicate pet policy verbally or via agency are not captured.

Delft 18-count verified via dual-path methodology: funda.nl/huur/delft/2+kamers/ clean URL and URL parameter search both return 18. A Funda UI display-lag bug causes the header count to show 26 until the page reloads with filters applied — the correct filtered count is 18, verified against filter-panel sub-counts.
